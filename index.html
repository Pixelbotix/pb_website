Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs, facilities, and services.

What age groups do your programs cater to?

Our programs are designed for students from ages 5 to 18, with age-appropriate curriculum and technology. We have four main programs: PixelBotix Brainy (ages 5-8), PixelBotix Little Techies (ages 9-12), PixelBotix Maker School (ages 13-15), and Future Innovators (ages 16-18). Each program is tailored to the developmental stage and interests of the age group.

Do you offer scholarships or financial assistance?

Yes, we are committed to making our programs accessible to all students passionate about technology. We offer merit-based scholarships and need-based financial assistance. Additionally, we partner with local schools and community organizations to provide sponsored spots in our programs. Please contact our admissions team for more information about eligibility and application process.

What qualifications do your instructors have?

Our instructors are a mix of industry professionals and educators with extensive experience in technology and teaching. All instructors have at minimum a bachelor's degree in a relevant field (Computer Science, Engineering, etc.) and undergo rigorous training in our teaching methodology. Many of our instructors also have advanced degrees and industry certifications. We maintain a low student-to-instructor ratio to ensure personalized attention.

How can schools partner with PixelBotix for lab setup?

Schools can partner with us through our Educational Partnership Program. We offer customized lab setup solutions based on the school's needs, budget, and curriculum requirements. The process typically begins with a consultation to understand your goals, followed by a tailored proposal. We handle everything from equipment procurement to installation and staff training. We also offer ongoing support and curriculum integration. Contact our partnerships team to schedule an initial consultation.

Do you offer virtual or remote learning options?

Yes, we offer virtual learning options for many of our programs. Our virtual programs use a combination of live instruction, interactive projects, and self-paced learning modules. We ship necessary equipment and materials to participants before the program starts. While we believe in the value of hands-on, in-person learning, our virtual programs are designed to be equally engaging and effective. They're particularly popular for students in remote areas or those with scheduling constraints.
